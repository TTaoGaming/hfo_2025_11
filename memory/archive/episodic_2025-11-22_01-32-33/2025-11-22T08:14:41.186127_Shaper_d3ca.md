### Access Control Lists (ACLs)
- **Definition**: ACLs extend traditional UNIX permissions (rwxrwxrwx) by allowing fine-grained access to files/directories for specific users/groups. Supported in filesystems like ext4 (POSIX ACLs), NTFS (Windows ACLs).
- **Review Methods**:
  - Linux/POSIX: `getfacl /path/to/file` or `ls -l` for basic perms.
  - Windows: `icacls /path` or `Get-Acl` in PowerShell.
  - Evidence: Misconfigured ACLs (e.g., world-writable root-owned files) can enable priv esc.

Using tool: I would read `/etc/fstab` to check mounted filesystems supporting ACLs, but simulating general fact: ACLs enabled via `mount -o acl`.
